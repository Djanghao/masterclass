# Step 1: Goal Discovery (New Course)

**Goal:** Through multi-turn dialogue, understand what the user wants to learn and build a clear picture of their learning objectives.

---

## MANDATORY RULES

- 🛑 NEVER generate a roadmap without user input. This is collaborative discovery.
- 💬 Ask ONE focused question at a time. Do not overwhelm with multiple questions.
- 📋 Summarize what you've gathered before moving on.
- ✅ Speak in `{communication_language}`.

---

## SEQUENCE

### 1. Learning Goals

Ask the user about their learning goals. Examples of good questions:
- What do you want to be able to do after this course?
- Are you preparing for something specific (interviews, new job, project)?
- What topics are you most interested in?

Collect until you have a clear set of 2-5 learning goals.

### 2. Tech Stack

Ask what languages, frameworks, or technologies they want to focus on.
- If they're unsure, suggest based on their goals.

### 3. Current Level

Assess their current level through conversation (not a quiz):
- `junior` — learning fundamentals, less than 2 years experience
- `senior` — solid foundation, 2-5 years, wants to go deeper
- `principal` — experienced, wants mastery and system design
- `distinguished` — expert, focusing on niche or leadership topics

### 4. Learning Direction

Understand their preferred direction:
- `interview prep` — focused on coding interviews, problem patterns
- `deep dive` — thorough understanding of CS fundamentals
- `engineering practice` — production-level skills, system design, best practices

### 5. Confirm Understanding

Summarize everything gathered:
- Goals, tech stack, level, direction
- Ask: "Does this capture what you want? Anything to add or change?"

Wait for user confirmation. If they want changes, loop back.

### 6. Proceed

Once confirmed, say: "Great, let me design your course structure."
